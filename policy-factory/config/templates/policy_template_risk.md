## 6. Risk Assessment Criteria

### Likelihood Scale

| Level | Likelihood     | Description        |
| ----- | -------------- | ------------------ |
| 5     | Almost Certain | > 80% probability  |
| 4     | Likely         | 60-80% probability |
| 3     | Possible       | 40-60% probability |
| 2     | Unlikely       | 20-40% probability |
| 1     | Rare           | < 20% probability  |

### Impact Scale

| Level | Impact       | Financial   | Operational            | Reputational      |
| ----- | ------------ | ----------- | ---------------------- | ----------------- |
| 5     | Catastrophic | > $1M       | Complete shutdown      | National media    |
| 4     | Major        | $500K-$1M   | Significant disruption | Regional media    |
| 3     | Moderate     | $100K-$500K | Moderate disruption    | Local media       |
| 2     | Minor        | $10K-$100K  | Minor disruption       | Limited attention |
| 1     | Negligible   | < $10K      | Minimal impact         | No attention      |

---

## 7. Risk Response Strategies

**For each identified risk, select appropriate response:**

- **Avoid:** Eliminate the risk by changing plans
- **Mitigate:** Reduce likelihood or impact
- **Transfer:** Shift risk to third party (insurance, outsourcing)
- **Accept:** Acknowledge and monitor the risk

---

## 8. Reporting and Escalation

**Risk Reporting:**

- Monthly risk dashboard to Risk Committee
- Quarterly comprehensive report to Board
- Immediate escalation for critical risks

**Escalation Triggers:**

- Risk exceeds tolerance level
- New critical risk identified
- Control failure detected
